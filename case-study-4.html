01 — The Problem

The hidden weight of new motherhood

The postpartum period — often called the "fourth trimester" — is one of the most physically and emotionally demanding phases of a mother's life. Yet postpartum depression remains widely under-diagnosed, and new mothers often lack accessible, non-judgmental support. Many feel isolated, overwhelmed, and unsure where to turn. Existing resources are fragmented, hard to find, and rarely feel personal. We set out to bridge the gap between Fourth Tri's in-person center and the daily lives of the mothers it serves.

Key Insight

"New mothers need more than information — they need a support system that meets them where they are, with tools that feel achievable even on their hardest days."

02 — Background Research

Understanding postpartum

Our research on postpartum depression illuminated its prevalence and profound impact on new mothers, revealing the urgent need for effective interventions. We explored four key areas — definition, information, credibility, and accessibility — asking questions like "Why do new mothers experience postpartum?", "When is intervention necessary?", "Are doctors educating this topic well enough?", and "How can we make the education accessible?" Recognizing the under-diagnosis and barriers to treatment, we embarked on developing a solution aimed at providing support and resources for affected mothers.

03 — User Persona

Who we're designing for

Our primary user is a new mother in the postpartum period — someone navigating the emotional and physical challenges of early motherhood while trying to stay connected to support systems. The app needed to feel like a gentle companion, not another task on her list.

04 — Brand Kit

Warm, soft, supportive

The FourthTri brand identity was designed to feel warm, nurturing, and approachable — like a soft landing into parenthood. The muted palette of dusty rose, deep plum, cream, and coral creates a sense of calm without feeling clinical. The logo features a mother-and-child silhouette, and every visual element — from the icons to the achievement badges — was designed to feel encouraging and gentle.

05 — User Testing

What mothers told us

We tested the app with new mothers and caregivers to validate our approach. The feedback confirmed that the warm, supportive tone resonated — users felt the app was on their side rather than adding pressure. Testers highlighted the achievements system and daily challenges as particularly motivating.

Key Takeaway

Mothers responded strongly to the supportive, non-judgmental tone of the app. The achievements and daily challenge features were the most praised — they gave users a sense of progress without adding pressure. Accessibility was flagged as an area for continued improvement.

06 — Hi-Fi Prototype

The final design

The final FourthTri app empowers mothers to visit the center, track their progress, earn achievements for daily challenges, and access support — all within a warm, inviting interface. The dashboard shows facility hours, scheduled classes like "Fireside Chats" and "Setting Boundaries 101," while the profile page celebrates milestones with badges for activities like Mom + Baby outings, healing, self-love, and more.
