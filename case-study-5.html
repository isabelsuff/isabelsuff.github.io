01 — The Problem

Sales training is stuck in the past

Traditional sales training is slow, expensive, and forgettable. Companies send teams to workshops or hand them PDFs, but when a real customer throws a curveball objection, most salespeople freeze. There's no way to practice the pressure of a live sales conversation without actually being in one — and by then, the deal is on the line. Sales managers need a tool that keeps their teams sharp between formal training sessions, and salespeople need a way to build confidence handling objections in real time, across any industry.

Key Insight

"You can't get better at handling objections by reading about them. You get better by responding to them — in the moment, under pressure, over and over again."

02 — Background Research

A wide-open market

Our competitive analysis revealed that there is virtually no direct competition in this space. Existing sales training tools focus on courses, videos, and role-playing with human partners — none offer real-time, AI-driven objection prompting that salespeople can practice with on their own. The closest alternatives are generic flashcard apps or expensive enterprise platforms that require full team onboarding. This gap confirmed a clear opportunity: build a lightweight, fun, industry-specific training tool that any company can adopt immediately.

Competitive Landscape

No direct competitors found. Existing tools are either too broad (generic learning apps), too expensive (enterprise role-play platforms), or too passive (video courses). Sales Qube occupies a unique position: real-time, gamified objection practice tailored by industry.

03 — User Persona

Who we're designing for

Sales Qube serves two users: the company leader who wants to keep their sales team performing at a high level, and the individual salesperson who wants to sharpen their skills. Our primary persona represents the decision-maker — the person who adopts the tool for their team.

04 — Brand Kit

Professional, bold, trustworthy

Sales Qube's visual identity balances professionalism with energy. The deep navy blue conveys trust and authority — critical for a B2B sales tool — while hot pink accents add urgency and excitement to key actions. The design system supports both light and dark modes, giving users flexibility. The cube logo uses a blue-and-pink geometric mark that feels modern and tech-forward. Clean white space, structured layouts, and industry-specific iconography make the app feel polished and enterprise-ready.

05 — The Final Design

Train anywhere, anytime

The final Sales Qube app walks users through a polished onboarding experience — from account creation with social sign-in options, to selecting their specific industry (Mortgage, Automotive, Solar, Retail, Entertainment, Finance, Health Care), to a premium upgrade flow with monthly and annual plans. The core experience delivers real-time objection prompts tailored to the user's industry, turning practice into a game. Premium unlocks unlimited cube spins, professional responses, client-simulated objections, and fresh content every session.
