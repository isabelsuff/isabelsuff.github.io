Case Study

GA Repository

An internal web tool for the Apple Developer Academy at Michigan State University — a centralized repository where staff can search, filter, and manage the hundreds of guiding activities created across cohorts and domains.

Timeline
2 Weeks
Location
Detroit, MI
Role
Designer
Tools
Sketch
01 — The Problem

Finding a needle in a haystack

The Apple Developer Academy creates a massive volume of guiding activities across multiple cohorts, journeys, and domains — from Challenges and Preludes to Coding and Design. Over time, these activities pile up with no centralized way to find, filter, or manage them. Staff members couldn't easily locate a specific activity, filter by year or cohort, or see what had already been created across domains. Valuable work was getting lost or duplicated because there was simply no system to navigate it all. We needed a clean, searchable repository that made the entire archive accessible in seconds.

Key Insight

"The activities existed — the problem was nobody could find them. A powerful search and filter system would unlock years of accumulated work."

02 — User Persona

Who we're designing for

Our users are Academy staff — mentors, program leads, and curriculum designers who need to quickly find existing guiding activities, reference past work, or add new content. They're busy, often working across multiple domains, and don't have time to dig through folders or ask colleagues where things are.

03 — Brand Kit

Dark, focused, functional

The GA Repository's visual identity mirrors the Apple Developer Academy's existing branding — a dark interface with crisp white content areas and a signature neon green accent for primary actions. The design is intentionally utilitarian: it's a tool, not a showcase. SF Pro Semibold keeps everything clean and readable, and domain-specific color coding (green for Design, yellow for Marketing, peach for Coding) helps users scan content at a glance. Author tags use distinct colors to make attribution clear.

04 — The Final Design

Everything in one place

The final design centers around three core experiences: a landing page with a prominent search bar and sidebar navigation organized by Journeys (Challenges, Preludes, Interludes, Epilogues, Foundations) and Domains (Coding, Design, M&M, Cross Domain); an "Add New" flow where staff can create new guiding activities with structured fields for domain, name, description, date, author, keynote uploads, and learning objectives; and a browsable archive organized by cohort with activity detail views showing key learnings, planning canvases, tags, and version history.

Landing page — search-first design with journey and domain navigation in the sidebar

Add New flow — structured form for creating guiding activities with all required metadata

Component system — sidebar states, cohort browsing, activity detail views, and metadata structure
