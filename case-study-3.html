What Dat Is?

A dictionary-style app that helps older generations learn and understand Gen Z slang — one word at a time — through definitions, pronunciation, examples, and a collectible badge system that makes learning feel like a game.

01 — The Problem

Lost in translation

Language evolves fast — and Gen Z slang moves even faster. Words like "rizz," "aura," "skibidi," and "bruh" have become part of everyday conversation for younger generations, but for anyone in their late 30s and beyond, it can feel like a completely different language. Parents, coworkers, and older family members find themselves confused, left out of conversations, or misinterpreting what's being said. There's no fun, accessible way to bridge the gap — Urban Dictionary is cluttered, and most resources aren't designed for someone who's genuinely trying to learn. We set out to build something that makes learning slang feel approachable, entertaining, and even rewarding.

Key Insight

"Older generations aren't out of touch — they just don't have a tool designed for them. Give them a dictionary that feels like a game, and they'll actually use it."

02 — User Persona

Who we're designing for

Our target user is someone in their late 30s to 50s who regularly encounters Gen Z slang — through their kids, social media, or coworkers — and wants to understand it without feeling embarrassed or out of the loop.

03 — Brand Kit

Cool, clean, approachable

The What Dat Is? visual identity leans into a cool periwinkle-blue palette that feels modern without being intimidating. All typography is lowercase SF Pro — keeping the tone casual and friendly, like a conversation rather than a textbook. Rounded corners throughout give everything a soft, approachable feel. The badge system uses bold, colorful hexagons with pop-culture imagery to make collecting words feel rewarding.

04 — The Final Design

A slang dictionary that slaps

The final app centers around three core experiences: a search-and-discover dictionary where users can look up any slang word and get its definition, pronunciation, and usage examples; a collectible badge system where learning words in specific categories unlocks colorful hexagonal badges like "Bodega," "Camp Couture," "Certified W," and "Drip Division"; and a words-found log that tracks everything you've learned with dates, so you can see your progress over time.

Home screen — search for any word, get the definition, pronunciation, and swipe for examples

Badge collection grid and detail view — unlock badges by learning words in each category

Words found — a running log of every word you've learned, with definitions and dates
