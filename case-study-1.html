Scroll Spike

Scroll Spike tracks your heart rate during screen time to reveal real-time health insights on how your phone use affects your well-being — helping Gen Z break the doomscrolling cycle.

01 — The Problem

Doomscrolling is a health crisis hiding in plain sight

Millions of people — especially Gen Z — spend hours each day mindlessly scrolling through social media, often without realizing the toll it takes on their mental, physical, and emotional well-being. Existing screen time tools report usage after the fact, but they don't connect the dots between scrolling behavior and its real-time impact on your body. Users dismiss these notifications as nagging and irrelevant. We saw an opportunity to make the invisible visible.

Key Insight

"Standard screen time notifications get swiped away and ignored. But when users see their heart rate spiking while scrolling, it creates an immediate, personal reason to put the phone down."

02 — Background Research

Uncovering the real cost of scrolling

Through our research, we uncovered compelling connections between screen time and its impact on mental, physical, and emotional well-being. We mapped out four key areas — mental health, physical health, stress response, and existing solutions — to understand the full picture. Social media use leads to FOMO, anxiety, and feelings of inadequacy. Physically, it drives fatigue, weight gain, and high blood pressure. Excessive scrolling raises cortisol and adrenaline levels, impairing the immune system over time. These insights played a key role in guiding us toward our finalized solution.

04 — Wireframes

Mapping the experience

During the wireframing stage, we were able to thoughtfully map out all of Scroll Spike's key functions and user needs. This process allowed us to conceptualize the overall structure and flow before diving into visual design — ensuring that every element served a clear purpose and aligned with our goals. We explored multiple layouts for the dashboard, onboarding flow, and notification system before landing on the direction that felt most intuitive.

Low-fidelity wireframes exploring dashboard layouts, heart rate displays, and onboarding flows

05 — Brand Kit

Meet Spike

Our brand kit was essential in ensuring consistency throughout every aspect of our work. By sticking to a unified set of fonts, colors, and our mascot Spike, we created a cohesive visual identity that tied everything together. Beyond aesthetics, the brand kit helped set the tone and overall vibe of our app — giving it a personality that users could connect with and recognize. 06 — User Testing

Notifications that actually work

User testing provided valuable insights into our notification design, confirming that we were on the right track. We compared standard screen time notifications against Scroll Spike's heart-rate-based alerts to see which approach resonated more. Participants responded well to the way we communicated urgency and the importance of heart health — striking a balance that felt motivating without being judgmental or too relaxed.

Key Takeaway

Standard screen time notifications were consistently dismissed — users described them as "judgmental" and "easy to ignore." Scroll Spike's heart-rate-based notifications stood out because they felt personal and urgent without shaming. Users said they'd actually engage with them, which validated our core approach.

07 — Hi-Fi Prototype

The final design

After iterating based on research and testing, we arrived at a polished high-fidelity prototype that brings together everything — the heart rate tracking dashboard, Spike's personality, the onboarding flow with goal-setting, and the notification system that users actually want to engage with. Every decision, from the dark UI that feels native to late-night scrolling to Spike's playful presence throughout, was informed by the process above.
